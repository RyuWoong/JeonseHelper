# Content Strategy

## Editorial Position

The guide should be practical, calm, and source-backed. It should not sound like marketing, fear content, or legal advice. When a topic depends on law, policy, or institution-specific procedure, the content should tell users where to verify the latest official requirement.

## Content Layers

1. Background knowledge: What jeonse is and why deposit protection matters.
2. Prevention: What to check before signing, on contract day, and after move-in.
3. Suspicion response: Situation-based roadmap for possible fraud.
4. Recovery process: Insurance claim, evidence collection, police report, court procedures, government support, and long-term tracking.

## Writing Rules

- Start each answer with the conclusion.
- Keep most answers to 2 to 5 sentences.
- Use first-level followups for common user confusion.
- Use deeper child questions for examples, warnings, documents, and decision criteria.
- Prefer official sources over blogs.
- Include `lastVerifiedAt` for policy or institution procedure.
- Avoid telling users that one path always works. Many outcomes depend on insurance status, contract timing, evidence, landlord behavior, property status, and official decisions.

## Source Policy

Official sources should be prioritized:

- Ministry of Land, Infrastructure and Transport
- Jeonse Fraud Victim Support Management System
- HUG, HF, SGI
- Supreme Court electronic litigation service
- Korean Legal Aid Corporation or government legal information
- Police and local government portals

Non-official articles may be used only for examples or experience context, not as final procedural authority.
